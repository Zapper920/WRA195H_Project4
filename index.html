Education

With the rising prices of college tuition, many graduating high school seniors have begun to wonder if alternative means of education. Although alternative means of education can be pursued, each option has its benefits, but also it's detriments. Below shows a comparison of the pros and cons of pursuing post-secondary education in graphic design.
Benefits of Attending a Formal Institution Detriments of Attending a Formal Institution Benefits of Self-Study Detriments of Self-Study
  • A cohort of students to study with
  • Decades of knowledge from professors
  • Access to the institution's resources
  • The monetary cost (the average cost of tuition is $13,677 in the US)
  • You are required to spend time, effort, and resources, while not being paid
  • It can be completely free
  • You can often enter the workforce much quicker
  • Can be difficult to find direction
  • Certain resources may not be available
There is no singular option for students; this major life choice requires delineation and a careful understanding of the consequences of either option. I found personally that the benefits of university outweighed the consequence of the cost. However, different lifestyles permit different options: the only correct decision is an informed one.

Industry

Employment Rates

The demand for digital designers was sparked largely by the “dot-com” bubble of the late 1990s to early 2000s. The early internet was a completely new frontier, and print designers eagerly capitalized on it. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the amount of graphic designers nearly doubled between 1999 and 2007, the height of the dot-com bubble. During this period, graphic design jobs increased at a much higher rate than the rate of overall employment. However, during the period of 2009-2013, the Graphic Design industry experienced a small recession. The dot-com bubble had burst, and investors and businesses alike found the internet as a much less lucrative venture. The industry has since been in a slight recession due to the current oversaturation of the field.

Employment Rates

Salary

Graphic Design wages have continuously risen over the past two decades. From the years 2000 to 2021, the annual mean wage rose from $38,000 to $60,000. When compared with the Consumer Price Index, a common means for calculating general inflation, wages have risen fairly proportionally to the rising price of goods. From 2019 to 2021, however, there has been a somewhat dramatic increase in wages relative to the rate of inflation. This suggests that graphic design could be a more advantageous career to enter in the coming years, if this trend is to continue. That being said, a similar increase can be seen from 1999 to 2001, only for the wages to then decrease the following year, and continue to follow the rate of inflation.

Career Paths

Currently, the field of graphic design offers a multitude of diverse career paths. Although the mean annual salary of designers in the US in 2021 was $59,970, only $1,690 over the average of all occupations, increased salaries can be found in different levels of career progression. Typically, the career path for a designer consists of junior, mid-level, and senior positions, requiring roughly <2, 2-5, and >5 years of experience respectively. In addition designers often move to managerial positions such as art or creative directors. In recent years, additional positions have opened as a result of the advent of digital technologies. UI/UX design and web development, for instance, are fields with a high rate of demand right now. With the cross-over and commonalities between graphic design and these fields, many designers are migrating to these positions due to their generally higher wages.
